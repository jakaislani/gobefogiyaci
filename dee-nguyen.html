Dee Nguyen Wiki, Age, Height, Weight, Boyfriend, Biography &amp; More

Dee Nguyen (born February 28, 1993) is a Vietnamese-born Australian Reality Television Star and Social Media Personality from Sydney. She is famous for being one of the winners of “The Challenge: War of the Worlds 2”.

It is the MTV reality competition series. Dee became the winner of the thirty-fourth season of “The Challenge” show. Furthermore, it is also the sequel to the War of the Worlds Season 33.

The Challenge: War of the Worlds 2 was released on the channel back on August 28, 2019. The other winners of the show are Jordan Wiseley (from The Real World: Portland), Chris “CT” Tamburello (from The Real World: Paris), and Rogan O’Connor from Ex on the Beach UK 2).

Earlier, Nguyen remained one of the contestants of the British television program called “Geordie Shore” Season 17 in 2018. Dee appeared in the series from 15 May 2018 to 31 July 2018. The reality TV star also participated in shows like “Total Madness” and “War of the Worlds”.

She got eliminated during the “Crash Landing” round during her participation in the War of the Worlds. Nguyen then retired for the War of the Worlds 2 and won $250,000 US Dollars.

Black Lives Matter Controversy

On June 08, 2020, Dee Nguyen got involved in a big controversy after making a harsh comment on the Black Lives Matter protest. As per the source, Bayleigh Dayton (fellow The Challenge contestant) shared a screenshot on social media where Dee made an inappropriate remark[1]Daily Mail.

She wrote on Twitter that she does not know why people think she is anti-BLM as she has been saying the same since the day she lost her virginity. However, the post later deleted from the platform.

Dee Nguyen was fired from The Challenge show after making anti-BLM remarks on June 09, 2020 (Dreshare.com)

On Instagram, one of her followers criticized her for continuously posting pictures. She replied by posting a black square for #BlackoutTuesday. The caption of the photo stated that people die every fu*ing day.

After the scandal, the makers of “The Challenge” declared that they fired her and completely cut the ties. The representative declared this news on Twitter by saying that they dismissed Nguyen for making offensive posts on the Black Lives Matter movement.

They also posted that they strongly condemn systemic racism. In addition, the reality TV star also realized her mistake and apologized to her audience on Instagram on June 09, 2020.

She wrote that she offers her sincerest apology to her co-workers to whom she has hurt due to her insensitive words. Dee even announced that she is taking a break from social media for a while in order to work on her mental health.

On May 25, 2020, George Floyd, a Black man, was brutally killed by a white police officer named Derek Chauvin. He choked him with his knee for nearly nine minutes. This incident led to various protests all over the USA.
